# Key References for the Article

## AI Adoption in Education (2024 Research)

### Recent Statistics and Studies

1. **RAND Corporation (2024)** - "Uneven Adoption of Artificial Intelligence Tools Among U.S. Teachers and Principals"
   - 25% of teachers using AI for instructional planning
   - 60% of principals using AI tools
   - Disparities by subject area and school poverty level
   - https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA134-25.html

2. **HEPI Student Survey (2025)** - "Student Generative AI Survey 2025"
   - 92% of university students using GenAI (up from 66% in 2024)
   - 88% using it for assessments (up from 53% in 2024)
   - https://www.hepi.ac.uk/2025/02/26/student-generative-ai-survey-2025/

3. **Pew Research Center (2025)** - Teen ChatGPT Usage
   - 26% of U.S. teens using ChatGPT for schoolwork in 2024
   - Doubled from 13% in 2023
   - https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/01/15/

4. **Frontiers in Education (2024)** - "Challenges and best practices in training teachers to utilize AI"
   - Systematic review identifying lack of teacher motivation as biggest challenge
   - https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/education/articles/10.3389/feduc.2024.1470853/full

## Participatory Culture and Informal Learning (Jenkins)

5. **Jenkins et al. (2009)** - "Confronting the Challenges of Participatory Culture"
   - MacArthur Foundation white paper
   - Defines participatory culture: low barriers, strong support for sharing, informal mentorship
   - https://www.macfound.org/media/article_pdfs/jenkins_white_paper.pdf
   - MIT Press book: https://direct.mit.edu/books/oa-monograph/3204/

6. **Jenkins, Ito, & boyd (2015)** - "Participatory Culture in a Networked Era"
   - Connected learning framework
   - Peer-driven knowledge networks
   - ISBN: 9780745660707

## EU AI Act and Regulatory Framework

7. **EU AI Act (2024)** - Regulation (EU) 2024/1689
   - First comprehensive AI legal framework worldwide
   - Education classified as high-risk domain
   - Entered into force August 1, 2024
   - https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai

8. **Annex III - High-Risk AI Systems in Education**
   - Learning outcome evaluation systems
   - Educational level assessment systems
   - Student behavior monitoring during tests
   - https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/annex/3/

## Technology Acceptance Models

11. **Davis (1989)** - "Perceived Usefulness, Perceived Ease of Use, and User Acceptance"
    - Original Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)
    - MIS Quarterly, 13(3), 319-340

12. **Venkatesh & Davis (2000)** - "A Theoretical Extension of the TAM"
    - TAM2: social influence factors
    - Accounts for 40-60% of variance in usefulness perceptions
    - Management Science, 46(2), 186-204

13. **Venkatesh et al. (2003)** - "User Acceptance of IT: Toward a Unified View"
    - UTAUT model integrating competing acceptance theories
    - MIS Quarterly, 27(3), 425-478

## Social Cognitive Theory and Self-Efficacy

14. **Bandura (1977)** - "Self-efficacy: Toward a Unifying Theory of Behavioral Change"
    - Foundational article on self-efficacy
    - Psychological Review, 84(2), 191-215

15. **Bandura (1986)** - "Social Foundations of Thought and Action"
    - Comprehensive social cognitive theory
    - Self-efficacy, observational learning, reciprocal determinism
    - Prentice-Hall

16. **Honicke & Broadbent (2016)** - "Academic self-efficacy: from theory to practice"
    - Meta-analysis showing self-efficacy as strongest predictor of achievement
    - Perspectives on Medical Education, 5(2), 76-92

    - Inventory Press, ISBN: 9781941753705

## Third-Person Effect

18. **Davison (1983)** - "The Third-Person Effect in Communication"
    - Original hypothesis: people perceive media affects others more than self
    - Public Opinion Quarterly, 47(1), 1-15

19. **Perloff (1999)** - "The Third-Person Effect: A Critical Review"
    - Review of 45 studies, all supporting perceptual component
    - Media Psychology, 1(4), 353-378

20. **Paul, Salwen & Dupagne (2000)** - "The Third-Person Effect: A Meta-Analysis"
    - Meta-analysis of 32 studies showing robust effect (r = .50)
    - Mass Communication and Society, 3(1), 57-85
